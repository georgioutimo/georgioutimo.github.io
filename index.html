The Piano

A piano is a string and percussion keyboard instrument that produces sound when a player presses keys, causing hammers to strike metal strings. The strings vibrate over a soundboard, and the intensity of the hammer's strike determines the loudness of the note, allowing for dynamic variation not possible on earlier instruments like the harpsichord. Invented by Bartolomeo Cristofori around 1700, the piano was originally called "pianoforte," meaning "soft and loud," and has since become a versatile instrument used in many musical genres, from classical to jazz and rock.
